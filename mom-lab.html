Lab Work

Mechanics of Materials Lab

This lab work focused on experimental mechanics, material behavior, stress and strain analysis, failure behavior, data processing, uncertainty, and engineering reporting. The work connected classroom mechanics concepts to physical testing and measured data.

Focus Areas

  • Stress, strain, and material response under loading.
  • Experimental testing and data collection.
  • Uncertainty analysis and comparison of measured results.
  • Technical communication through structured engineering lab reports.

Tools Used

Material testing equipment, MATLAB, Excel, data analysis, technical writing.

Engineering Skills Developed

  • Interpreting experimental data and comparing it to theoretical expectations.
  • Documenting procedures, results, uncertainty, and limitations clearly.
  • Explaining material behavior through measured evidence.
  • Connecting mechanics of materials theory to real test results.

What I Learned

This lab strengthened my ability to work with real engineering data, communicate uncertainty, and explain how material behavior changes under different loading conditions.
