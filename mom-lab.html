Overview

This lab sequence strengthened my ability to move from raw physical measurements to engineering conclusions. Across the reports, I worked with strain gauges, DAQ systems, LabVIEW virtual instruments, Instron mechanical testing, uncertainty propagation, material property extraction, ImageJ measurements, and pressure-vessel theory.

The work was valuable because it connected theory to real test data. Instead of simply applying equations, each report required decisions about sensor setup, calibration, measurement uncertainty, failure mode interpretation, assumptions, and how confidently the data supported a conclusion.

Why This Lab Matters

Aerospace and mechanical systems depend on more than perfect textbook assumptions. Real hardware has noise, tolerances, imperfect boundary conditions, uncertain material properties, setup limitations, and failure modes that need to be interpreted carefully.

These reports helped me build the mindset required for real engineering work: measure carefully, understand the limitations of the setup, quantify uncertainty, compare independent methods, and explain what the data can and cannot prove.

Lab Reports

Lab 1 DAQ / Strain Gauges

Uncertainty Analysis of Strain Gauge Measurements Using Digital Data Acquisition

Used strain-based measurements to estimate the outside radius of a stool footrest and compared the result against a direct geometric circumference measurement. The experiment emphasized DAQ resolution, sampling window selection, signal amplification, Wheatstone bridge behavior, and 95% uncertainty comparison.

Core idea Resolve small strain signals from voltage measurements.
Engineering focus Measurement quality, amplification, and uncertainty propagation.

Skills: LabVIEW, DAQ, strain gauges, Wheatstone bridge circuits, RSS uncertainty

Lab 2 Beam Bending / Calibration

Strain-Instrumented Cantilever Beam Scale

Built and evaluated a strain-instrumented cantilever beam as a low-cost scale for estimating bottle weight and average gulp size. The report compared a calibration-based voltage-to-weight method against a Mechanics of Materials beam-bending model, using Monte Carlo and RSS methods to evaluate uncertainty.

Core idea Use strain response to estimate applied load.
Engineering focus Calibration-based measurement compared against an analytical beam model.

Skills: beam bending, calibration, strain gauges, Monte Carlo uncertainty, LabVIEW

Lab 3 Material Characterization

Failure Testing of Material Specimens

Used displacement-controlled tensile and compressive testing to develop stress-strain relationships for an unknown metal, carbon fiber, nylon, and plaster of Paris. The report extracted material properties, compared ductile and brittle behavior, evaluated specific strength and stiffness, and identified the unknown metal as most consistent with 7075-T6 aluminum.

Core idea Turn load-displacement data into material behavior.
Engineering focus Failure, material identification, and stress-strain interpretation.

Skills: Instron testing, stress-strain analysis, material identification, Ramberg-Osgood modeling

Lab 4 Adhesive Joint Failure

Failure Analysis of Adhesives

Evaluated the shear strength of four adhesives in 6061-T6 aluminum double-lap joints using measured failure load and post-failure adhesive area. The report used calibrated ImageJ measurements, failure-mode observations, load-path assumptions, and uncertainty comparison to evaluate adhesive performance.

Core idea Calculate adhesive shear strength from failure load and failed bond area.
Engineering focus Joint strength, failure mode, bonded-area uncertainty, and load-path assumptions.

Skills: adhesive joints, ImageJ, shear stress, failure analysis, uncertainty comparison

Final Project Pressure Vessels / Strain Measurement

Internal Pressure of a Carbonated Beverage Can

Estimated the internal pressure of an unopened carbonated beverage can using two independent approaches: strain-gauge measurements aligned with hoop and longitudinal directions, and a diameter-change method after opening. The work applied thin-walled pressure-vessel theory, plane-stress relationships, DAQ measurement, and uncertainty propagation.

Core idea Convert measured strain into internal pressure.
Engineering focus Pressure-vessel theory, strain measurement, and independent-method comparison.

Skills: strain gauges, DAQ, pressure-vessel theory, uncertainty propagation, technical writing

What I Took Away

The biggest takeaway from this lab sequence was learning how much engineering judgment sits between raw data and a final conclusion. The calculations matter, but so do sensor placement, calibration quality, noise, uncertainty, boundary conditions, failure observations, and whether the model assumptions are actually reasonable for the test.

This is the kind of work that connects directly to real mechanical and aerospace engineering: testing hardware, understanding how it fails, quantifying confidence in the result, and communicating the conclusion clearly enough that another engineer can trust it.
