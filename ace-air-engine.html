Project

ACE Air Engine

The ACE Air Engine project involved hands-on mechanical design, machining, manufacturing, and assembly of an air-powered engine. The project helped build practical experience with tolerances, component fit, machining workflow, and mechanical system assembly.

Project Goals

  • Machine and assemble components for an air-powered engine.
  • Understand how tolerances and part quality affect mechanical motion.
  • Develop hands-on experience with manufacturing processes and equipment.
  • Connect design intent to physical part production and assembly.

Tools Used

CNC machining, manual machining, measurement tools, assembly tools, engineering drawings.

Skills Developed

  • Reading and interpreting engineering drawings.
  • Understanding machining workflow and setup.
  • Inspecting parts for fit and function.
  • Assembling a mechanical system from manufactured components.

What I Learned

This project strengthened my appreciation for manufacturability, tolerances, and the practical difference between a CAD model and a working physical assembly.
