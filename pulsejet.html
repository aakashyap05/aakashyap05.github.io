Project

Valveless Pulsejet Engine

This project focuses on the design and analysis of a Lockwood-style valveless pulsejet engine concept. The work combines CAD modeling, SolidWorks Flow Simulation, and MATLAB calculations to study geometry, airflow behavior, resonance estimates, and early performance trends.

Project Goals

  • Design a manufacturable valveless pulsejet engine geometry.
  • Use SolidWorks Flow Simulation to evaluate cold-flow behavior.
  • Use MATLAB to estimate thrust trends, resonance behavior, and performance sensitivity.
  • Develop a test bench plan for future physical testing.

Tools Used

SolidWorks, SolidWorks Flow Simulation, MATLAB, CAD, CFD, technical documentation.

Work Completed

  • Created and refined CAD geometry for the intake, chamber, and tailpipe.
  • Set up cold-flow CFD studies to evaluate air movement through the engine body.
  • Developed MATLAB calculations for early thrust and resonance estimates.
  • Compared design choices such as tailpipe diameter, intake geometry, and chamber volume.

What I Learned

This project helped me connect mechanical design, fluid dynamics, simulation setup, MATLAB modeling, and engineering judgment in a system where geometry strongly affects expected performance.
